Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This dataset collects information from 100k medical appointments in Brazil and is focused on the question of whether

or not patients show up for their appointment. Several of characteristics of the patient are included in each row.

Be careful about the encoding of the last column: it says ‘No’ if the patient showed up to their appointment, and ‘Yes’ if they did not show up.

we investigate this dataset to find why do 30% of patients miss their scheduled appointments?

Question(s) for Analysis:¶

1- Does receiving an SMS affect showing up for the appointment?

2- Is there a correlation between having a scholarship and showing up for the appointment?

3- Is there a correlation between having Hypertension and showing up for the appointment?

4- Is there a correlation between having diabetes and showing up for the appointment?

5- Is there a correlation between drinking alcoholism and showing up for the appointment?

6- Is there a correlation between having a handicap and showing up for the appointment?

7- Is there a correlation between gender and showing up for the appointment?

8- Is age affect showing up for the appointment?

9- Is age and having diseases affect showing up for the appointment?

10- Is age and having handicaps affect in showing up for the appointment?

Conclusions¶

The result I get from my analysis:

The number of patients who showed up for the appointment and didn't receive an SMS is greater than those who received an SMS so receiving an SMS doesn't affect showing up for the appointment.

The patients who had not a scholarship showed up for the appointment than those who had it so having a scholarship doesn't affect showing up for the appointment.

The number of patients who showed up and didn't have hypertension was more than the number of patients who showed up and have hypertension but also we have to consider that the number of patients who did have not hypertension was almost 4 times the number of patients who have hypertension so having hypertension doesn't affect showing up for the appointment.

The number of patients who showed up and didn't have diabetes was much more than the number of patients who showed up and have diabetes but also we have to consider that the number of patients who did have not diabetes was about 13 times the number of patients who have diabetes so having diabetes doesn't affect showing up for the appointment.

The number of patients who showed up and were not drinking alcoholism was much more than the number of patients who showed up and were drinking alcoholism but also we have to consider that the number of patients who were not drinking alcoholism was about 32 times the number of patients who were drinking alcoholism so drinking alcoholism doesn't affect showing up for the appointment.

The patients who didn't have any handicaps were much more than The patients who have one handicap or more so it's normal that the patients who didn't have any handicaps were the most showed up for their appointment so having handicaps doesn't affect showing up for the appointment.

Females are more than males in either showing up or not showing up so we can not say that gender affect showing up for the appointment.

Patients who have from 0 to 10 and from 50 to 60 were the most attended patients, on the other hand, patients who have 65 years old and more are the least attended the appointment. maybe we can say that patients who have 65 years old and more are likely to not attend the appointment but we can not say that age has that effect on showing up for the appointment.

Most of the patients who have the two diseases (Hypertension, Diabetes) and of age above 60 didn't show up.

The patients who have three handicaps and are of age under 55 years old are the most showed up compared to other cases, and the patients who have two handicaps and are of age above 50 years old are the least showed up compared to other cases.

Limitations¶

In my analysis I didn't consider the appointment day.

The data has to include more information about the appointment, its type, the time that the appointment takes to end that may effect on showing up.
